Sam Pittman Career

Sam Pittman, born on November 28, 1961, has had a distinguished career in American football, particularly as a coach. His journey began as a multi-sport athlete in high school, eventually leading him to play defensive end at Pittsburg State University from 1980 to 1983. Pittman's playing career culminated with him being named a National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA) All-American in his senior year. In 1998, Pittsburg State honored his contributions by inducting him into their Athletic Hall of Fame.

Transitioning to coaching, Pittman demonstrated his natural affinity for the profession. He started as an offensive coordinator at Beggs High School in Oklahoma and later took on head coaching roles at Princeton Junior-Senior High School in Missouri and Trenton High School in Missouri during the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Pittman's coaching journey then expanded to the college level, where he became the offensive line coach at Hutchinson Community College in Kansas in 1991. His coaching career evolved with positions at various universities, including Northern Illinois, Cincinnati, Oklahoma, Western Michigan, Missouri, Kansas, and Northern Illinois once again. Pittman's expertise in coaching offensive lines became evident, and his ability to connect with people, showcased during recruiting trips, earned him a reputation as one of the top recruiters in the country.
