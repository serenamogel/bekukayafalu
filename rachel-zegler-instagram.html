Biography of Rachel Zegler

Rachel Anne Zegler, an American actress born on May 3, 2001, gained widespread recognition for her remarkable portrayal of María in Steven Spielberg’s acclaimed musical adaptation, “West Side Story” in 2021. Her brilliant performance in the film earned her the prestigious Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture Comedy or Musical. Zegler further showcased her talent by taking on the role of Anthea in the superhero movie “Shazam! Fury of the Gods” released in 2023.

Zegler’s journey into the entertainment industry began while she attended Immaculate Conception High School in New Jersey. During her time there, she starred in various musical productions, including notable roles like Belle in “Beauty and the Beast,” Ariel in “The Little Mermaid,” and Dorothy Brock in “42nd Street.” Her exceptional performances in these productions earned her nominations for the Metro Award in the category of Actress in a Leading Role.

Apart from her stage work, Zegler also gained attention through her YouTube channel, which she started in July 2015. One of her notable videos features her rendition of “Shallow” from the movie “A Star Is Born,” amassing over 12.2 million views on Twitter.

Zegler’s rise to prominence reached new heights when she responded to Steven Spielberg’s open casting call for the role of Maria in “West Side Story” via Twitter. Despite being just 16 years old at the time, she impressed Spielberg with her renditions of “Tonight” and “I Feel Pretty.” Out of over 30,000 applicants, Zegler was chosen for the coveted role, marking her film debut. The movie was released in December 2021 and received critical acclaim. For her exceptional performance, Zegler received a Critics’ Choice Movie Award nomination and made history as the youngest winner of the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture Comedy or Musical, as well as the first actress of Colombian descent to achieve this feat.

In addition to her notable roles, Zegler is set to star as Snow White in a live-action adaptation of Disney’s animated film, slated for release in 2024. She is also cast as the lead character in the animated musical film “Spellbound” and will portray Lucy Gray Baird in the film adaptation of “The Hunger Games: The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes.” Zegler’s talent continues to attract attention, and she has exciting projects on the horizon, including her involvement in Kyle Mooney’s directorial debut film, “Y2K,” and the third installment of the “Paddington” film series.

Beyond her professional endeavors, Zegler has been in a relationship with fellow “West Side Story” co-star Josh Andrés Rivera since 2019.

Rachel Zegler’s remarkable achievements have earned her accolades and recognition. She was listed as one of the top 35 actors under 35 by The Ringer’s The Big Picture podcast and was included in Forbes’ 30 Under 30 class of 2022. Her talent and dedication to her craft continue to captivate audiences and establish her as a rising star in the entertainment industry.
