Gina Lollobrigida Death Cause And Obituary

The “most beautiful woman in the world,” an Italian Actress, has passed away. The age of Gina Lollobrigida was 95.

The Associated Press reported that Lollobrigida’s death was initially announced by the Italian news agency Laprise.

Governor Eugenio Giani of Tuscany, where Lollobrigida was made an honorary citizen, confirmed her passing.

According to Reuters, her former attorney confirmed her passing at a clinic in Rome. According to Sky News, he did not indicate the reason for her death.

According to her IMDB biography, Lollobrigida was well-known for the movies “Crossed Swords,” “The Hunchback of Notre Dame,” and “Beautiful but Dangerous.”

Throughout her career, the Actress won 33 foreign awards, had seven further nominations, and was nominated for one BAFTA Award. According to IMDB, she was awarded a star on the Walk of Fame in 2018.

She appeared in films with legends like Humphrey Bogart, Frank Sinatra, Rock Hudson, and Errol Flynn as “La Lollo.”

Lollobrigida “made Marilyn Monroe appear like Shirley Temple,” according to Bogart.

After leaving her cinematic career, Lollobrigida turned to politics and photography, according to BBC News.

According to Reuters, the former Actress ran for and lost a seat in the Italian parliament for a left-leaning political Party last year.

Gina Lollobrigida Family

Gina was a little secretive as she had not revealed many details about her family before her death, so very little about them is known.

She is one of four sisters, the other three being Giuliana, Maria, and Fernanda, as far as we know.

In 1949, Lollobrigida married Milko Kofi, a physician from Slovenia. Their only child, Andrea Milko, was born on July 28, 1957.

With “Kofi” and their son Andrea, Lollobrigida relocated to Toronto, Ontario, Canada, from her home in Italy in 1960. The pair sadly got divorced in 1971.

She made her engagement to Javier Rigau y Rafols, a 45-year-old Spanish businessman, public in October 2006, when she was 79 years old.

They had become friends after meeting at a Party in Monte Carlo in 1984. On December 6, 2006, the engagement was canceled due to the pressure of heavy media attention.

Lollobrigida and Rigau wed in Spain in 2006 after agreeing to a prenuptial contract.
