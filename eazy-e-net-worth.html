Eazy E: Lifestyle & Vacation

Lifestyle

E was born in Compton, Los Angeles, California, United States.

Growing up, E’s father was a postal worker, and his mother was a grade-school administrator.

Similarly, because of the rough neighborhood, he supported himself mainly by selling drugs and also introduced his cousin to the illegal occupation.

He was nicknamed “thug” because of his tiny stature.

However, at the age of 22, E’s cousin was shot and killed. That’s where he decided to quit drug dealing and start Hip-hop.

And finally, in the mid-1980s, he started recording songs in his parents’ garage.

That is when he started working on his blockbuster record “Ruthless Records.”

Eazy-E made his demise on 26th March 1995 from AIDS-induced pneumonia, one month after his diagnosis.

After E’s death, his daughter Ebie launched a crowd-funding campaign to produce Ruthless Scandal: No More Lies to investigate her father’s death.

E has eleven children in total with eight different women.

Charity

The family of late rappers launched Eazy Street Kids Charity.

It aims to raise a fund for underprivileged youth and the local school system.

Likewise, his charitable nature benefited organizations such as Athletes and Entertainers For Kids, United Colors, and the Make a Wish Foundation.

Similarly, he supported causes like Health, Education, HIV & AIDS, Hunger, Poverty, etc.

Eazy E: Movies, Songs, Books, Investments & Endorsements

After Eric died in 1995, many books, movies, and biographies on him has been released.

Movies

There are more than 15 movies made on the life of Eazy.

In 2012 Ruthless Propaganda released documentation on the life of Eric.

The documentary was named after his first label “Ruthless Label.”

Furthermore, the documentary involved interviews between E’s manager Jerry Heller, MC Ren, etc.

E was one of the most controversial figures in gangsta rap.

Similarly, in 2015, a film named “Straight Outta Compton” was released.

Actor Jason Mitchell played Eazy’s role, and the entire movie is dedicated to his memory.

In 2016, American biographical television named “Surviving Compton: Dre, Suge & Michelle” was released.

E’s role was played by Omari Wallace, which portrayed the feud between Eazy E, Dr. Dre, and Michelle.

Apart from that other films and documentaries released on him include Eazy-E: The Life and Timez of Eric Wright, Tupac: Resurrection, N.W.A.: Straight Outta Compton Film, The Miracle Mile Shot, and And Ya Don’t Stop: Hip Hop’s Greatest Videos Vol. 1.

Songs

Eric has a total of 3 songs listed on the Billboard.

Eazy’s career began in 1987 through N.W.A.

On 6th November 1987, the group released N.W.A. and the Posse, which was certified gold in the United States.

Similarly, in 1988, Eazy released his debut album “Eazy Duz It,” featuring 12 tracks.

The album was able to sell 2.5 million copies in the United States and was later ranked at 41 number on the Billboard 200.

He later got nominated as Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop Album, Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop New Artist for the album.

In 1989, rapper Ice Cube left N.W.A, and the group continued as a four-piece ensemble.

In 1990, they released “100 Miles and Runnin,” and the diss war started between N.W.A and Ice Cube.

Eventually, in 1994, N.W.A began to split up after Jerry Heller became the group’s manager which initiated the feud between Eazy and Dr. Dre.

Eazy has influenced artists like Ice T, Redd Foxx, King Tee, Egyptian Lover, Schoolly D, Too Short, George Clinton, etc.

Book Publications

On 13th September 2016, Ben Westhoff wrote a book on E’s life.

The book was named ” Original Gangstas: The Untold Story of Dr. Dre, Eazy-E, Ice Cube, Tupac Shakur, and the Birth of West Coast Rap.“

The book generated $55 million in revenue.

Investments & Edorsements

According to Eazy’s manager Jerry Heller, E had invested the first $250,000 and would eventually put up to $1 million into the company for Ruthless Labels.

Moving further, Eazy endorsed many brands such as Nike, Reebok, Zara, etc.

Career

Before starting his career as a rapper, Eric was involved in drugs.

He used to earn well from it. However, after his cousin’s death, he decided to turn his life around and start fresh as a rapper.

His hip-hop career began with N.W.A. However, it was Dr. Dre who spent a lot of time convincing him to became a rapper.

In 1990, E’s song Efil4zaggin hit No. 1 on the Billboard charts.

Every single bandmate confirmed that E was way ahead of his time as he managed to pay studio bills all by himself.

In 1994, E also became a host of a Los Angeles radio music show on KKBT-FM.

Moreover, he released his first solo EP, 5150 Home 4 tha Sick, in 1992 and followed it up with another EP the following year.

Unfortunately, in 1995, the talented rapper passed away due to Pneumonia and HIV AIDS.

At the time of his death in 1995, he was working on his album.

After his death, his wife Tomica Woods was able to keep the “Ruthless Record.”

E is buried at Rose Hills Memorial Park in Whittier, California.

More than three thousand people attended his funeral.

And finally, after ten months of Eric Lynn Wright’s death, his final album Str8 off tha Streetz of Muthaphukkin Compton was released.
